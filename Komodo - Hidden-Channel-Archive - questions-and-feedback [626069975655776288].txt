[27-Jan-20 06:54 AM] Audo#5667
**Discord Channel Structure Proposal**

The Komodo Community Portal fills many gaps we have tried to fill with Discord.

The problem has been that content gets lost on Discord, so we have given each topic a channel to make it easier to scroll up and find the relevant messages and pinned posts. The result has been an enormous list of channels that overwhelms any newcomer.

Now that we have the portal, we should take another look at the Discord channel structure and try to find ways to make its use more efficient.

**Reasons For Restructuring**

1) We want to encourage the discussion to happen on the portal instead as it provides us with long term benefits.
2) With fewer channels, we would concentrate the conversation and make the Discord feel more active.
3) It is hard to stay on-topic in live chat, so why don't we accept that and use few more general channels instead?

**Share Your Thoughts**

Read the proposal and answer the poll on the Komodo Community Portal.

- Yes, I agree we should reduce the number of channels and rethink the structure
- No, the current Discord structure works, and we should keep it as it is

Share your thoughts here or on the portal thread.

**_About our moderation policy_ **

We are an open-source project, and we have to keep things as open and transparent as possible. There is a line there somewhere, though. It's not up to the Komodo team or community moderators to decide where that line is. Our moderation policy is a reflection of our culture and the input we receive.

Here is the bottom line:

1) Community Moderators enforce our community guidelines & moderation policy
2) **Community influences the guidelines & moderation policy**
3) Moderator team reviews and implements changes.

Current banning policy: we ban scammers and people who can't behave (situation that happened on #üìà‚îÉtalk-trading does not apply).

If our community feels we are too loose on the moderation policy, then we need to have some opinions and discussion about it. We are not going to change the policy ad hoc. 

The best place to leave feedback and discuss moderation policy is this channel.

As far as I see all Cosmos chains can freely use the same PoS security, but don't receive automatic validation of that security from the main chain, but they have the option to do so. Cosmos uses a limited set of validators that reach consensus for transactions
